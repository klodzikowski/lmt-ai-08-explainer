MNIST Network Explainer

Draw a digit and watch it travel through our course network — 784 pixels in, 64 hidden neurons in the middle, 10 probabilities out. The weights were trained on 60,000 real handwritten digits (MNIST), score 97.7% on 10,000 digits the network never saw, and are baked into this one file: everything below is the real network doing real arithmetic, live in your browser.

Step 2What the network sees

Your drawing is shrunk, centred and turned into a 28 × 28 grid — just 784 numbers between 0 (black) and 1 (white). This list of numbers is the only thing the network receives.

middle row of pixels: —

Step 3Hidden layer — 64 neurons

Each square is one neuron (8 × 8 = 64). Every neuron looks at all 784 pixels, multiplies each by its own learnt weight, adds them up, and fires if the total is positive. Brighter = firing harder.

Hover over a square to inspect one neuron.

Step 4The verdict — 10 probabilities

Waiting for a digit…

Ten output neurons, one per digit, converted to percentages (softmax). They always add up to 100% — the network must place a bet, even on an empty canvas.
